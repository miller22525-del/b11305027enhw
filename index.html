My Journey in Film: In Front of and Behind the Camera

Hello, I'm Jammy Wamg, the Director of Lumifilm Studio. Before recruiting new partners, I want to share my personal history with film production, which will provide insight into how we view 'filmmaking'.

Outside of academics, one of my favorite interests has always been participating in film projects. Starting in high school as the female lead in a campus film, that experience was my first time in front of the lens. It was then I realized that 'acting' is far more than just memorizing lines. Later, I contributed to research-related film projects in my university department. Each shoot presented a different challenge, teaching me lessons that classrooms could not.

Initially, I thought acting was simpleâ€”just remember the script and perform. However, once filming began, I discovered that body language is immensely important in performance. Some great actors can convey entire emotions and stories with just a glance, a posture, or a small gesture. On set, many smooth, natural-looking scenes are actually the result of repeated adjustments, discussions, and retakes. Every revision, every re-enactment, deepened my understanding that acting is an art requiring observation, comprehension, and dedication.
